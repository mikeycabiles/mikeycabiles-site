The one-person content ops sprint I now run every Monday

April 21, 2026 · 9 minute read

I used to start Mondays by reacting to whatever felt urgent. That gave me activity, but not momentum. So I rebuilt the start of my week into a system that gets me to publishable content in 90 minutes.

The stack

  • One AI research prompt that pulls trend + audience signal + product relevance.
  • One content map template that forces me to pick angle, format, and CTA before writing.
  • One distribution checklist that repackages one core idea across channels.

The point is not volume. The point is consistency with context. Every post should reinforce positioning, not just fill a calendar slot.

If your weekly system takes willpower every time, it's not a system yet.

What changed after three weeks

Publishing got easier. Quality improved. Most importantly, I stopped guessing what to talk about. The anxiety of "what should I post?" basically disappeared.
